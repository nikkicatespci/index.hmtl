Legal Protection

12 Documents to Review Before You Sign

By Nikki Cates, Founder — Pure Compass Investments  |  2026

Most legal trouble doesn't start with a bad decision. It starts with a signature on something nobody read closely, because reading it closely felt like a luxury at the time.

That's the gap Pure Compass Shield exists to close. Legal review has historically been treated as something you earn access to once you can afford a retainer. It shouldn't work that way, and it doesn't have to.

Here are twelve categories of documents that deserve a second set of trained eyes before your signature goes on them.

1. Purchase and sale agreements. Whether you're buying a home, a car, or a business, this document sets every term of the deal, including what happens if something goes wrong.

2. Leases. As a tenant or a landlord, the lease decides who's responsible for what, and most disputes trace back to language nobody clarified up front.

3. Quitclaim deeds. Common in family property transfers, these move ownership interest without any guarantee the title is actually clear.

4. LLC operating agreements. This is the document that determines whether your business entity actually protects you, or just looks like it does.

5. Contractor and service agreements. Scope, payment terms, and lien protections live here, and vague language is where disputes are born.

6. Loan documents and promissory notes. Interest terms, default conditions, and personal guarantees can carry consequences far beyond the loan amount.

7. Partnership agreements. What happens if a partner wants out, stops contributing, or disagrees with a major decision should be answered before you start, not during the conflict.

8. Employment offers and severance agreements. Non-competes, arbitration clauses, and severance terms are negotiable more often than people realize.

9. Non-disclosure and non-compete agreements. These can outlast the relationship that created them and restrict you longer than you'd expect.

10. Powers of attorney. This document hands someone legal authority over your decisions. Its scope and limits matter enormously.

11. Wills and trusts. The most consequential document many people never have reviewed, because it only seems urgent after it's too late to fix.

12. Insurance policy declarations. Exclusions and coverage limits are often buried in language designed to be skimmed past, not read.

None of these require a crisis to justify a look. They require access, which is exactly what Pure Compass Shield was built to provide: ongoing legal protection that doesn't depend on your income bracket or your ability to pay by the hour when something goes wrong.

Before you sign, pause.
